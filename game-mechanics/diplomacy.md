# ☮️ Diplomacy

## Peace

Peace is the standard diplomacy, and it is also your relationship with every country at the start of the game. Peace means that you guys can buy each other's trade offers, but can't go over the other's land or take it. You should be at peace if you're not at war or in coalition with the others, unless there are some special circumstances I will explain in ROW.

## Right of Way

Giving a nation the Right of Way is allowing them to walk in your land without declaring war. This is very risky, as if you are invading someone else, they can stack troops in your homeland and take over with a click of the war button. These are 3 circumstances where you can have a ROW with someone.

1. After leaving a coalition, your relationship with a country will go from shared intelligence to ROW. Unless you trust them, you should change it back to peace
2. If you are invading the same country as someone else, you can consider giving ROW, so you guys will not accidentally declare war on each other. However, you should change it back to peace after the war to make sure they don't use it to their advantage
3. If you are playing with a friend, but in different coalitions, you guys can have ROW and go over each other's land for faster expansion

Overall, you should avoid giving a long-term ROW to other players, as many use it to directly invade you, and it is a big safety hazard for your country, unless you're sure that they won't betray you!

## Shared Intelligence

This allows the other player to see all your units, what you are constructing, mobilising, and invading (basically all aspects you can see). Unless you have Security Counsel (P2W), you only have shared intelligence with people if you're in the same coalition. Sometimes, you can use this to your advantage. You can invite a player you want to invade into your coalition, take photos of his units, and invade him right after kicking him. This gives you a clear idea of the other player's strength.

## War

This is when you and the other nation are at war, meaning you conquer each other's lands and can't accept the other's trade orders. If you invade a country, your relationship would instantly switch from Peace to War

> Your [troop does not stop\*](../getting-started/interface.md#move) if another country takes it, which means you can trigger an invasion from going in the other's province, changing your relation to war. If you want to return to peace with the nation, start a conversation with the nation and click New Trade, where you can adjust the trade from Peace, ROW, and War. Offer a Peace for Peace and wait for them to accept.

## Managing Diplomacy

### Who should you go to war with?

#### Rank

If you want to invade someone, you should check their rank first. Usually, go for someone who is Rank 1-15 if you are very new, and gradually move up to the 16-30 range if you are confident. If you are an experienced player, you can also go for someone ranked 30-40. Anything above is considered a tough battle unless they are afk or you have a lot more troops and good builds than they have.

#### Builds

After you check their rank, you should check their builds. You can judge builds with 4 types.

1. Bad: No Builds to 1 Army Base Players: Go for it. They are either afk or noobs with no big units, so you should go after these
2. Starting: Some army base, maybe a recruiting office, and arms industry, but no real expansion: Mainly new players. You can go for them after a few days, once you build up some strong troops.
3. Intermediate: Army Base level 2, Air Base Level 2, Naval Bases, etc. But not very advanced builds like Army Base lvl 3-5, generally no 3-5 lvl for any build. Go for them middle to late game. Should expect a bit of a fight, but you should finish them in a reasonable time period
4. Advanced: Lvl 5 builds, usually a high-ranked player. Avoid going to war with these unless it is a big coalition war, where you are forced to.&#x20;
